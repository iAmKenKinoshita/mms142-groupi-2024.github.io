According to Shaikh et al. (2024), the practice of Social Media Influencer (SMI) Marketing primarily relies on the concept of social learning theory; it implies that influencers act as guides that prompt their audience and followers to adopt behaviors and the products associated with them.

This notion is supported by the greater level of trust that those familiar with particular influencers have over more generally known celebrities, demonstrating a relative effectiveness that SMIs possess for marketing (Gräve, 2017).

Although, due to this increase in effectiveness achieved through familiarity, influencers tend to have a more limited reach in comparison to established celebrities as a result of only catering to specific niches.

Relatability refers to the tendency of consumers to trust SMIs that have, or at least appear to have, similar experiences and interests as they do; and inversely, find it harder to perceive credibility from influencers that they do not consider relatable.

In 2024, however, with influencer marketing becoming one of the fastest growing industries in the marketing space, influencer's in the upper echelons of the distribution are gaining a significant income through brand deals and advertising revenue (Oberlo, n.d.).

Macro and Mega-influencers earn
hundreds of thousands of dollars
annually due to their reach and fame (Dublino, 2024).

This has drastically affected the lifestyle of these content creators, and in extension, the lives that their followers perceive through their portrayal on social media. Wealth is developing into a double-edged sword for SMIs as relatability turns into a declining commodity.

For the younger generations specifically, Allende, Hallengren, & Berenguer (2024) observed in their study that teenagers are growing insecure due to the luxurious lives that most popular influencers lead. In terms of marketing, consumers of SMI content are also generating an awareness for these types of influencers and deciding to opt out of following them; deeming them untrustworthy.

Growing Insecurity

Consequently, the more paid collaborations a content creator does, the less their followers consider them to be credible and genuine (Hahl, 2023). This makes it difficult for marketers and advertisers to consistently do campaigns with top performing SMIs; on the side of the creators, it is difficult not to expect them to take more brand deals in order to garner more wealth.

These issues are mitigated by AI-Driven Virtual Influencers by removing the factor of personal gain from the SMI. The artificial SMIs do not experience the pressure of needing to produce more paid collaborations, allowing for the perception of authenticity to be maintained from their audience.

Managing Authenticity

Granted, there is the immediate difficulty of creating a sense of genuineness from a digital machine; however, it may be counteracted by the ability to control the narrative and lifestyle of these AI models. Through deliberate planning and presentation of a humble yet interesting virtual life, followers can build a level of relatability to these SMIs and eventually grow to trust them as if they were human influencers.

"Tension equals attention" is the new "sex sells" in the landscape of internet and social media influencer marketing. In the paper "The Influence of YouTube Beauty Vloggers’ Drama on The Profit of Cosmetics Brand" (Xiong, 2024), drama on content creator-driven platforms such YouTube has been found to be a major motivator for generating impressions or social capital. Through conflict, a sort of parasocial rapport is built by those following the events towards the influencers; creating biases for those involved, strengthening their respective communities and investing more trust in those they support (Christin & Lewis, 2021).

As concluded in the previously mentioned study, a positive correlation can be observed between profits from paid collaborations and the perpetuation of tension between SMIs, based upon the greater reach and intrigue that these controversy-powered creators have over their peers. This has led marketers to depend on these large channels and accounts for their promotional campaigns to achieve the most amount of clicks and views they can manage for a brand deal.

Despite its effectiveness, this marketing strategy has

With anecdotal sentiments from users on the internet echoing a growing disdain for drama and controversy, it paints this picture that marketers and social media influencers are effectively disregarding a large portion of their market.

The Issues in Adapting to AI
Influencers in Marketing Media

Starting within the generation of text and images through generative AI, various groundbreaking trends in marketing have emerged within society: the emergence of AI influencers. These AI-driven personas take on the role of social media personalities, creating brand-aligned content and engaging in real-time interactions with followers. With AI-driven marketing campaigns, exemplified by AI digital influencers, remain relatively new to the public eye, growing concerns within the public have emerged about its misutilization that sidelines human creators in favor of AI influencers.

With generative AI continuously evolving to replicate human-made persona with greater accuracy, its cost-effectiveness and alignment with brand goals have led some companies to prioritize it over human media creation and engagement. This shift has fueled a growing backlash against the use of such technology with consumers raising skepticism about the credibility and authenticity of the content and engagement these AI influencers create and share in social media.

In general, social media influencers build their prominence online by authentically showcasing their personality and crafting content that aligns with the lifestyle or narrative they present.

This authenticity forms the foundation of their online success, as audiences are drawn to the relevance of their lifestyle content.

Progressingly, their ability to share lifestyle content and engage followers, cultivating trust and authenticity within their respective communities, enables influencers to turn their online presence into a profession with brand collaborations.

However, AI influencers lack the human essence that establishes human influencers.

AI influencers cannot genuinely experience the brands they promote or feel the emotions needed to connect authentically with consumers, fostering a sense of mistrust towards the AI influencers and the brands they represent.

ABSENCE OF HUMAN ELEMENT

Advancing transparency has become a key foundation of influencer marketing, with influencers creating and sharing relatable media content about the products or services they promote through brand deals, thereby building trust between consumers and the brand itself.

However, skepticism has arisen regarding the ethical transparency of AI influencers, as they are unable to genuinely experience the products or services they endorse, progressing a mistrust toward the marketed brand (Andrus, 2024).

The limitations presented in these marketing initiatives can negatively affect both the trust and reputation of the brand as AI influencers lack the human ability to thoughtfully evaluate the products or services they promote.

It is within this point that leads consumers to question the brand's credibility, establishing a disconnection among the consumers between the AI influencers and the brand.

The marketing campaign would then be deemed as inauthentic and solely profit-driven, further damaging the trust and credence in the brand's reputation.

QUESTIONING BRAND IDEALS

Correspondingly, the lack of transparency in AI influencers contributes to the widespread dissemination of misinformation, causing the general public to question the factuality and authenticity of the media content they post.

YouGov statistics report that 75% of people in the UK have expressed concern that the rampant spread of misinformation is majorly caused by digitally altered content (Dixon, 2024).

It is within this that fostered a negative public perception that AI-related initiatives, specifically AI influencer marketing, are harmful to society due to its groundless and unsubstantiated marketed material.
