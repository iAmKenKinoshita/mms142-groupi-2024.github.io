Disruptive Technology

A product is considered disruptive when mainstream users of a product from incumbent companies switch to a product from new entrants.

Defining the term

Disruptive

These dominant companies often concentrate on specific areas, overlooking other aspects of their business. This oversight creates opportunities that small businesses can exploit.

In 1995, Harvard Business School professor Clayton Christensen coined the term disruptive technology which he elaborated in his book The Innovator's Dilemma in 1997.

However, in the subsequent work titled The Innovator’s Solution, disruptive innovation superseded the term disruptive technology.
